Cloud Seeding: An Inturlude

An interesting issue I came across while researching cloud seeding:

How do you tell the truth? Take for example this quote from an Saudi Arabian newspaper (2016):

Desalinating one cubic metre of water from the sea costs $60, whereas the same amount of water extracted through cloud seeding costs just $1.

Sounds too good to be true right?

Here’s a quote from a consulting company (~2015) on the cost of desalination plants:

The average costs, represented by the best fit line in the data shown, are about $0.70/m3.

So who’s telling the truth?

Here’s a quote from the US Bureau of Reclamation (2007):

The California DWR has estimated that an additional 300,000–400,000 ac‐ft of new supply could be realized by seeding, with an investment of around $7 million (DWR 2005). This represents a cost of about $19 per ac‐ft.

For the metric-minded, an ac-ft is about 1300 m3.

Wait, that’s a cost of 0.015 $ per m3!?

What do you do when three separate, reliable sources disagree completely?






























There is no resolution here, only suffering lol
